## The Two Loops

**The Spiral** (الصراط المستقيم — The Straight Path):
Help with genuine intention → good outcome → reinforced good intention → help again → better outcome → repeat forever.

> "Cooperate in righteousness and piety." — Quran 5:2

**The Anti-Spiral** (الفساد في الأرض — Corruption on Earth):
Extract for personal gain → short-term gain → reinforced selfish intention → extract more → collapse → find new host → repeat until nothing is left.

> "Do not cooperate in sin and aggression." — Quran 5:2

One builds. One consumes. One is sustainable across infinite time. The other always collapses.

> "Every child is born upon fitrah (natural goodness)." — Hadith (Bukhari & Muslim)

The default state of every human is good intention. The framework returns you to what you already are.

## The Test

**One week.** Every day:
- Morning: Check your intention for the day
- During: Help one person with genuine intention
- Evening: What happened? Was the outcome good?

Seven days. Measure results against your life before.

If your life is better — the spiral works. If it isn't — discard this framework.

> "There is no compulsion in religion." — Quran 2:256

---

## Falsifiability

This framework fails if:
- One week of genuine helping intention produces worse outcomes than extraction
- Cooperative communities consistently underperform extractive ones over time
- Civilizations built on cooperation collapse faster than those built on extraction
- The feedback loop does not reinforce (helping does not lead to more helping)
- Children raised in spiral environments turn out worse than those raised in extractive ones

Each claim is testable. Each layer is independently verifiable. If the outcomes don't hold, the framework is wrong.
